Critical Finding: 73% of branch transactions are cash-based

The dominant share of branch staff time is consumed by low-value manual cash handling. 12 branches exceed 75% cash ratio, with 4 above 80%. This is the single largest drag on staff productivity — releasing this capacity through digital/cashless migration would free FTE time for advisory, consumer lending, and client activation. The target is not more manual transactions, but fewer.

Target Logic: Linked to Module 1 (Market Share) & Module 2 (Product Mix)

Methodology & Data Notes

Perimeter: 35 outlets, 31 benchmarked (excl. 1 ghost, 2 Sporteli, 1 structural outlier Mitrovice Veriore). FTE = total branch employees from Branch Efficiency MERGED source.

  • No role-level FTE breakdown: Source data provides only total FTE per branch. All "per employee" metrics (PI, Micro, CIB, transaction) use total branch FTE as denominator. This is consistent across all branches, making relative comparison valid, but absolute per-role productivity cannot be computed.
  • FLOW vs STOCK: Loans 2025, new accounts, transactions = FLOW. OnBalance, deposits, active clients = STOCK. Never divide stock by flow counts.
  • Cash ratio: (Cash withdrawals + Cash deposits) / Total branch transactions. Proxy for teller burden intensity.
  • Target derivation: Recovery branches (M1 PI pen < 13.1%) get higher client acquisition and consumer lending targets. Branches with high TDA dependency (M2 > 50%) get deposit diversification targets. Branches with high cash ratio (> 75%) get digital migration targets. All targets anchored to peer-group median with uplift.
  • Missing daily data: 3 branches (1001, 100504, 1007) show 0.0 for daily metrics — treated as data gap, not zero productivity. Excluded from daily-metric charts.
  • Cross-sell intensity: (Total loans + New accounts) / Active PI clients. Proxy, not true cross-sell from product-holding data.
  • Module linkage: M1 groups (Recovery/Acceleration/Defend) based on PI penetration vs 13.1% median and 18% target. M2 flags (Push Consumer, Moderate TDA, Push POS) drive product-specific targets.
