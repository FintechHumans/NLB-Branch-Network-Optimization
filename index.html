Prishtina City-Level Context
At city level, Prishtina stands at 21.4% PI penetration (48,619 active clients across 227,466 population). This means the city is not underpenetrated overall; the issue is internal branch allocation and outlet role clarity, not market weakness. Four active outlets and one ghost branch (100101, 7 FTE, zero output) share this market. Individual branch pen% in Prishtina, Prizren (10.9%), and Mitrovice (18.5%) should be read as internal allocation indicators, not as standalone market-share measures.

Methodology — Population Penetration in Multi-Branch Cities

Three cities operate with multiple NLB Prishtina outlets sharing the same municipal population base: Prishtina (5 outlets, pop. 227,466), Prizren (2 outlets, pop. 147,246), and Mitrovice (2 outlets, pop. 64,742).

In these cities, individual branch PI penetration is calculated as branch active clients ÷ full city population. This structurally deflates branch-level pen% and makes these branches appear weaker than they are.

  • City-level penetration is the correct measure for cross-market comparison (e.g., “Is Prizren underpenetrated vs Peje?”)
  • Branch-level penetration within multi-branch cities is used only for internal allocation decisions (e.g., “Is 0001 Prishtine carrying its fair share of the city’s client base?”)

City-level PI penetrations: Prishtina 21.4%, Mitrovice 18.5%, Prizren 10.9%. At city level, Prishtina and Mitrovice are at or above target. Prizren remains the most underpenetrated major city.

Structural outlier: Mitrovice Veriore (100703) shows 116% penetration — active clients exceed registered population due to cross-municipality client registration. Excluded from all benchmark charts and scoring.

Business accounts: Active business account counts are estimated (penetration ratio × registered businesses). Inactive business counts are derived from business active ratio. Treat as directional, not exact.

Methodology — Module 2: Product Mix, Funding & POS

Consumer loan penetration = consumer loans disbursed (2025 flow) / active PI clients. This measures sales intensity into the existing base — not portfolio weight.

Deposit mix is PI-level only (CA, TDA, Savings from Summary sheet). Micro and CIB deposit components are from segment sheets and estimated via avg-per-employee * FTE. Total deposits = PI + Micro + CIB.

POS data is from Branch Productivity.xlsx "POS Business" sheet — 2025 terminal counts by branch. Density = POS / registered businesses * 100.

M-Banking adoption is from "Digital Penetration PI" sheet — M-click users / active PI clients.

Cross-sell intensity = (total loans + new accounts) / active PI clients. This is a proxy — not a true product-per-client count.

  • No pricing/rate data is available in the source files. All funding-cost logic is directional (TDA > SA > CA cost hierarchy) rather than rate-based.
  • Business active/inactive counts are estimated from penetration ratios — treat as directional.
  • POS = 0 for 5 branches: 1001 Prishtine, Prizren 2, Prishtine-Llapit, Bregu i Diellit, Mitrovice 2. These may reflect data gaps or genuine absence of merchant acquiring in those outlets.

Critical Finding: 73% of branch transactions are cash-based

The dominant share of branch staff time is consumed by low-value manual cash handling. 12 branches exceed 75% cash ratio, with 4 above 80%. This is the single largest drag on staff productivity — releasing this capacity through digital/cashless migration would free FTE time for advisory, consumer lending, and client activation. The target is not more manual transactions, but fewer.

Target Logic: Linked to Module 1 (Market Share) & Module 2 (Product Mix)

Targets are NOT derived solely from current output. They reflect: (1) market-share gap from M1 — Recovery branches need higher PI acquisition targets; (2) product-mix priorities from M2 — branches weak in consumer lending or POS need commercial push targets; (3) cash-to-digital migration — branches with high cash burden get transaction-reduction targets, not transaction-increase targets; (4) peer-group benchmark — targets anchored to peer median with uplift for high-opportunity branches.

Methodology & Data Notes

Perimeter: 35 outlets, 31 benchmarked (excl. 1 ghost, 2 Sporteli, 1 structural outlier Mitrovice Veriore). FTE = total branch employees from Branch Efficiency MERGED source.

  • No role-level FTE breakdown: Source data provides only total FTE per branch. All "per employee" metrics (PI, Micro, CIB, transaction) use total branch FTE as denominator. This is consistent across all branches, making relative comparison valid, but absolute per-role productivity cannot be computed.
  • FLOW vs STOCK: Loans 2025, new accounts, transactions = FLOW. OnBalance, deposits, active clients = STOCK. Never divide stock by flow counts.
  • Cash ratio: (Cash withdrawals + Cash deposits) / Total branch transactions. Proxy for teller burden intensity.
  • Target derivation: Recovery branches (M1 PI pen < 13.1%) get higher client acquisition and consumer lending targets. Branches with high TDA dependency (M2 > 50%) get deposit diversification targets. Branches with high cash ratio (> 75%) get digital migration targets. All targets anchored to peer-group median with uplift.
  • Missing daily data: 3 branches (1001, 100504, 1007) show 0.0 for daily metrics — treated as data gap, not zero productivity. Excluded from daily-metric charts.
  • Cross-sell intensity: (Total loans + New accounts) / Active PI clients. Proxy, not true cross-sell from product-holding data.
  • Module linkage: M1 groups (Recovery/Acceleration/Defend) based on PI penetration vs 13.1% median and 18% target. M2 flags (Push Consumer, Moderate TDA, Push POS) drive product-specific targets.
